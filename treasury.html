Treasury

What the Treasury is

The Treasury is the engine that makes TSX sustainable. It collects a portion of ecosystem fees and reinvests them to strengthen long-term stability and resilience.

Where Treasury funding comes from

From the base transaction fee system. TSX is designed to generate ongoing cash flow through real usage.

  • 1.0% → Treasury (core engine)
  • 1.0% → Stability & External Assets (separate bucket that supports long-term resilience)
  • 0.5% → Controlled re-circulation
  • 0.5% → Creator (operations + personal income without dumping)

Treasury allocation model (locked concept)

Conceptual allocation (transparent, no leverage):

  • 25% → TSX buybacks & burns (deflation pressure over time)
  • 25% → Stable assets (includes future Raise support)
  • 25% → Broader markets (BTC, SOL, ETFs)
  • 25% → Hard assets (gold, silver)

Rules of the Treasury

  • No leverage (no debt, no risky compounding)
  • No promises (no guaranteed returns)
  • Transparency-first (clear reporting and public posture)

Why this matters

The goal is a token that strengthens itself over time instead of relying on hype. The Treasury is how TSX earns stability and durability—one transaction at a time.
